About Me

I have worked for nine years in the GIS field and currently, I am a GIS Analyst at the Citrus Research Board. Previously, I worked as a GIS Analyst at Quad Knopf, an engineering consulting firm. I have also worked as a Biogeographic Research Analyst doing GIS with the California Department of Fish and Wildlife (CDFW) and as a GIS Technician with an environmental consulting firm whose clients included private developers, government agencies and county and municipal governments. I have gained experience in providing GIS services to these clients.

Experience:

  • Produced maps for EIRs for several projects, such as the Millerton EIR in Fresno County and the SacPort Regional Petroleum Terminal Project in Sacramento
  • Digitized the storm drain utility layers for the City of Exeter's update of its utility maps
  • Suitability Analysis - For the Dinuba Reclamation-Conservation-Recreation project, I provided a suitability analysis in which the proposed golf course would be on land reserved for agricultural use under the Williamson Act. Because of this analysis, the City of Dinuba was able to satisfy the Department of Conservation that the site was the only one suitable for the project and the agricultural designation was removed, thus allowing the project to move forward.
  • Land Use Feasibility Study - For the Friant Corridor Study, I produced maps for a land use feasibility study which would identify natural and manmade factors that could be assets, opportunities, or incentives to future land uses.

Education:

  • Completed an Associate in Science degree in GIS at American River College, Sacramento, California
  • Completed online courses and received the Postbaccalaureate Certificate in Geographic Information Systems from Penn State University

Strengths:

Skills

Spatial Analysis

Spatial analysis tools can be used to solve problems, such as how far is a business or a residence from the flood zone, what is the best location for an ice cream business, what are the crime patterns in a certain neighborhood, the spatial distribution of where customers live in proximity to a pizza place, or who are a business's competitors and their locations.

Map Production

I have produced both large- and small-scale maps, mapbooks, maps dealing with general plan updates, biological maps, maps for environmental impact reports, and maps for a land use feasibility study according to design principles of cartography, layout, coordinate systems, and map scales. I have digitized spatial data sets and geographic features for these maps.

Site Selection

In selecting a new site for a business, a map displaying the target area and the demographic information needed to help in the site selection decision is much more effective than documents and spreadsheets containing numbers and charts. For example, GIS can show the distance within a one-mile radius the potential customers for the new location of a pizza shop.

Web Mapping

Web mapping is the technique of utilizing maps on the Internet that are obtained by a geographical information system for spatial data and is a major new trend in GIS. In order to keep my GIS skills up-to-date, I have enhanced my GIS skills with web mapping skills utilizing HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, and Carto. Examples of my web maps are presented below.
